# Plain-Language Summary for Kirill

Fracture is the right next design wave. The simulator can already create and modify rare quarterstaves, but it cannot yet model the step that permanently protects one valuable modifier. That protection is central to the intended physical-quarterstaff route.

The reliable core is simple: a Rare quarterstaff with at least four installed explicit modifiers and no existing fracture may select one eligible modifier instance at random and mark it fractured. Nothing else changes. Later accepted removal or replacement operations must continue to treat that modifier as immutable.

The important boundary is equally clear. Current sources disagree about or do not fully expose Revealed Desecrated interactions. M46 does not guess. A later M46-A should fail closed whenever a Desecrated modifier or unrevealed placeholder is present. Ordinary modifiers and crafted/Essence explicit modifiers remain in the clean candidate pool; the latter is supported by the general official wording and current player evidence but remains project-model, not server truth.

This package is only the design and source-verification record. Claude audits next; no Fracturing Orb can execute yet.
