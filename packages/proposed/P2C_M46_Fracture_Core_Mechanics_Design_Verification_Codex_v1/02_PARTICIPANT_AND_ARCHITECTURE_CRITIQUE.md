# Participant and Architecture Critique

## Boundary conclusion

M46 Fracture is the correct next move and the proposed boundary is right-sized.

- It advances the actual quarterstaff crafting route instead of adding another infrastructure layer.
- The base operation is a single atomic flag mutation over an installed modifier instance and fits the existing canonical `ItemState`, exact path identity, replay, resolver, and admission architecture.
- The official/current source contour is stronger than the remaining special-state disputes: Rare input, at least four modifiers, random modifier, no already-fractured input, and locked result are consistently stated.
- Desecrated/Revealed ambiguity can be isolated by a fail-closed clean-core boundary rather than blocking Fracture entirely.

## Material correction to the prepared YAML

The current `fracturing_orb` row is a useful candidate description, not accepted mechanics. Its broad target plus a special disputed Revealed rule must not be copied directly into runtime. M46-A should instead define an explicit clean candidate pool and reject all Desecrated/Revealed placeholder states until PD-013 has a separate decision.

## No material objection

There is no material reason to choose more chain hardening or another operation before this design. Fracture adds a route-critical capability, reuses accepted state primitives, and remains automatically testable without introducing planning or optimization.
