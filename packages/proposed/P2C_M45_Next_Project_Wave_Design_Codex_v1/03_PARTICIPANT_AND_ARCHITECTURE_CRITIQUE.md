# Participant and Architecture Critique

## Material objection to a Fracture-first interpretation

A clean Fracture core is mechanically smaller than a full Omen layer, but it is not the highest-value next move for the accepted product lane. `config/project_scope.yaml` explicitly starts from `fractured_crit_quarterstaff`, states that the item already has the required fracture, and excludes modelling how the fracture was obtained. Fracture should be admitted when alternative starting routes are opened, not merely because it is the simplest unimplemented currency.

## Material objection to more chain infrastructure

M43-A already evaluates caller-supplied fixed sequences of one through eight accepted operations. More chain hardening now would add infrastructure without giving the route a new meaningful crafting action. Search preparation would also risk drifting toward planner/optimizer behavior before the operation surface is mature.

## Why the Omen boundary is right-sized

The accepted M38 resolver already has a non-admitting modifier interface shape. Accepted add, remove, remove-then-add, Perfect Essence, Alchemy, exact, Monte Carlo, replay, and atomic multi-add kernels supply most of the execution machinery. A clean Omen batch therefore adds product behavior through composition rather than duplicated currency functions.

The boundary must not be “all Omens”. Repository data and current external catalogues are not identical, and several effects depend on unaccepted Desecrated/Jawbone/Reveal mechanics or underspecified multi-remove rules. M45 should define one independent modifier architecture and a pinned clean allowlist; everything else remains fail-closed.

Conclusion: M45 is strategically correct when scoped to the clean accepted-currency Omen core. It is broader than a single-Omen microstep, but narrower than a generalized meta-crafting algebra.
