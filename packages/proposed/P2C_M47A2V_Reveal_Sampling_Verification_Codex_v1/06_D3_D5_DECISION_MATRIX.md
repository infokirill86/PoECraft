# D3-D5 Decision Matrix

| Question | Evidence needed to accept candidate | Evidence that rejects candidate | Inconclusive evidence | Later explicit decision |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| D3-A guaranteed compatible exclusive | Known-compatible homogeneous setups, no zero-exclusive counterexample, no trusted-source conflict | One verified successful window with a compatible exclusive pool and zero exclusive offers | Compatibility not independently established or setup varies | ChatGPT/User accepts D3-A or selects another model |
| D4-A tier-row sequential weighted sampling without replacement | Complete eligible tier-row pools and weights, homogeneous repeated setups, no structural conflicts, pre-registered comparison favoring D4-A | Offer outside eligible pool; forbidden family/group conflict; repeatable systematic mismatch to the pinned model | Missing pool/weights, mixed setups, or insufficient power to separate family-first alternatives | ChatGPT/User accepts D4-A, chooses another project model, or requests more capture |
| D5-A fail closed when fewer than three compatible offers exist | Repeatable predicted-insufficient setup fails, item/placeholder unchanged, currency not consumed | Currency consumed, state changed, reduced offer set shown, or constraints relaxed | Failure seen but post-state/consumption not captured | ChatGPT/User accepts D5-A or selects observed behavior |

## Decision discipline

- A structural counterexample can reject a candidate immediately after capture validation.
- A lack of counterexamples is not automatic acceptance.
- Statistical comparison must be pre-registered before the follow-up capture, not tuned after viewing results.
- Claude audits the evidence package; ChatGPT/User alone changes project-model truth.
